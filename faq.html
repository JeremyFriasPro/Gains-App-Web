FAQ

How long are the competitions?
Competitions are 1 to 2 minutes long.
What kinds of competitions and exercises are available?
Competitions are either based on time or max reps. If it’s timed then it means who can finish the challenge in the shortest time. If it’s max reps then it means who can do the most reps before failure or in the allotted time. All exercise routines are welcome as long as it fits the 1- 2 min timed or max rep criteria.
I am currently a personal trainer, what I can I do on GainsApp?
As a personal trainer, you can judge competitions and collect a fee for each event. Your fee is a pre-set % percentage of the competition pot that you’re judging. You get to also create the challenges that competitors choose to compete In. These challenges then become digital assets that you own and the basis of your value. Lastly, you can create tokens that represent fractional ownership of yourself. Other users can invest in you if they purchase your share tokens and In return they receive a dividend payout.
My fitness level is not advanced at all, is there something I can do on GainsApp?
Yes. You can either make bets on other users who you believe can win competitions or invest in them. If you make an investment in a competitor or a judge, you will receive a payout into your wallet every time they earn. You can also search and find challenges that you feel are suitable for you or direct message of the trainers to get you ready for your next competition.
How does GainsApp benefit the fitness community?
It keeps everyone connected, active, accountable and earning money doing what they love to do for free.
What’s the difference between a bet and an investment on GainsApp?
A bet on GainsApp is when you place a wager on competitor(s) who are competing in upcoming competitions. If your person wins then you receive a PAYOUT. An investment is when you buy and hold tokens that represents fractional ownership in competitors and/or judges. The token gives you rights to future payouts from the competitors and/or judges. You make money whenever they make money. You can trade or resell the tokens with other users.
I already have a strong following as a fitness influencer on social media. Why should I join Gainsapp?
It gives your audience another opportunity to continue supporting you and being rewarded for that support. Other platforms are sided and set up to only enrich the 1 party (the seller). On GainsApp, we go beyond likes and comments. When your audience believes in you, they will invest in you and they can hold that digital asset in exchange for future dividends. You receive upfront income for their investment. Similar to personal trainers, you can judge competitions and collect a fee for each event. Your fee is a pre-set % percentage of the competition pot that you’re judging. You get to also create the challenges that competitors choose to compete in. These challenges then become digital assets that you own and the partially basis of your value on GainsApp. Lastly, you can create tokens that represent fractional ownership of yourself.
Can I be a judge and a competitor on GainsApp?
Yes you can. The judge user role is the only user type that will require approval from GainsApp. At any time you can be a competitor or a spectator. You can cannot compete or wager in challenges that you created.
How do payouts work on GainsApp?
Each user that joins GainsApp will set up a digital wallet. There is a payout and end of each competition and your portion will automatically go into your gainsapp wallet.
Can I bet on myself?
Yes. All Competitors bet on themselves to enter each competition.
What does it mean to buy share tokens on GainsApp?
This is similar but NOT the same- to buying shares into a company and if the company value grows, your shares go up I’m value. If the company turns a profit, they share it with you. When you buy tokens on GainsApp, you’re buying shares in your favorite competitor or judge and you’re entitled to a payout every time they earn money from competitions. You can buy more, trade and resell the tokens at any time to other users.
Is there a limitation on how many times I can compete, judge or bet?
NO. Your participation is at your discretion.
